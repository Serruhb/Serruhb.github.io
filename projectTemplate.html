The Problem

The company’s biggest blocker to AI-assisted coaching wasn’t a lack of data—it was a lack of reliable resolution paths. Customer issues were solved every day, but the “how” was rarely documented in a structured way. Resolutions lived in scattered case comments, Jira threads, and one-off conversations.

As a result, Customer Success teams spent significant time re-discovering answers. Time-to-context was high, first-contact resolution was inconsistent, and there was no clean foundation to build deep search, sentiment-aware suggestions, or in-call coaching on top of.

Phase 1: The SYNaTRA Solution

Capture, grade, and refine resolution paths so they can become the backbone of future coaching and training.

SYNaTRA is a synthetic triage assistant that retrieves and summarizes customer history from Salesforce Cases, Jira tickets, and Snowflake subscription data into a single, concise view. It parses an issue description, searches across systems using SOQL, JQL, and SQL, and returns an actionable triage summary with recommended resolution paths.

Crucially, SYNaTRA also creates a feedback loop: agents can compare the assistant’s recommendations to the resolution they actually executed. That feedback is captured and scored, turning each interaction into a data point that refines future recommendations and prepares the organization for deeper AI coaching in later phases.

Key Features · Phase 1

How SYNaTRA assembles context, proposes resolution paths, and captures feedback for future coaching.

UC

Unified Customer Context

Searches Salesforce for Accounts, Cases, Contracts, and Subscriptions, and Snowflake for subscription details, then builds a concise customer overview with active plans, status, and total MRR.

Data sources: Salesforce (Case, Contract, Account), Snowflake subscription tables.
IS

Issue-Aware Deep Search

Converts short issue descriptions into keyword strings and constructs SOQL and JQL queries. Searches Salesforce Cases and Jira issues for relevant historical tickets within a 12-month window to discover prior resolutions.

Tech: SOQL, JQL, optional SOSL.
RP

Resolution Path Recommendations

Generates ≤150-word summaries of historical resolutions, promotes the strongest patterns into recommended resolution paths, and labels each with confidence and similarity scores plus source references (e.g., SF-Case-12345, JIRA-6789).

GF

Grading & Feedback Loop

Allows agents to rate whether SYNaTRA’s recommended path matched the resolution they actually performed, capturing variance, gaps, and “better than suggested” outcomes to refine future recommendations.

GR

Guardrails, Access Rules, and Safety

Uses integration-user credentials for Salesforce and Jira, masks PII (emails, phone numbers, tokens) in summaries, and avoids persistent storage beyond the current response. Designed for internal use only with role-aware visibility.

System Architecture

How SYNaTRA orchestrates connectors, skills, and feedback into a triage report.

SYNaTRA orchestrates multiple connectors and skills to transform a short issue description into a structured triage report and feedback signal:

  • Inputs: issue_description (free text) and optional customer_identifier (AccountId, ContractId, internal CustomerId).
  • Customer Identification: Salesforce account search skill with fuzzy matching and top-5 candidates when only a name is provided.
  • Keyword & Query Builder: Tokenizes issue text and builds JQL and SOQL/SOSL keyword queries to search Jira and Salesforce history.
  • Record Fetching: Salesforce Cases & Contracts, Jira tickets, and Snowflake subscription data (12-month lookback).
  • Summarization & Scoring: Resolution summaries plus similarity and confidence scores at both record and overall result levels.
  • Insight Assembly: Final message composed into a readable chat response for Slack, MS Teams, or Workbot, including recommended resolution path(s) and quick links.
  • Feedback Capture: Lightweight interface for agents to rate recommendation quality and indicate the actual resolution path taken.

If Salesforce or Jira are unavailable, SYNaTRA responds with a user-friendly message: “I couldn’t reach Salesforce or Jira right now — please try again shortly or check your connection settings.”

Roadmap: Phased AI Coaching Strategy

SYNaTRA is Phase 1 of a multi-phase initiative to move from “better triage” to “in-call AI coaching.”

Phase 1 · SYNaTRA

Capture & Grade Resolution Paths

Aggregate customer context, recommend resolution paths, and capture feedback on what agents actually did. The goal is to transform messy, undocumented resolutions into structured, graded paths that can fuel future search, training, and coaching.

Phase 2

Deep Search & Sentiment-Aware Suggestions

Expand from triage to true deep search: use the captured resolution paths as a knowledge base, layer in richer customer context, and begin modeling basic sentiment and risk indicators.

SYNaTRA and Genie collaboratively suggest tailored resolution paths based not just on the issue type, but on customer history, tone, and likelihood of satisfaction.

Phase 3

Training Agent & Simulated Calls

Use transcribed calls where recommended resolutions led to satisfied customers to train a “coach-in-training” agent. The Genie can mock customer scenarios, listen to reps, and simulate calls around common issues and successful resolution patterns.

At the end of each training session, targeted questions collect rep feedback so the training agent can be actively tuned and improved.

Phase 4

In-Call Coaching

Deploy in-call coaching informed by the refined resolution data and Phase 3 training. Start with the most common, best-understood call types, then expand coverage as the model and knowledge base mature.

During live calls, Genie surfaces suggested paths, risk signals, and next best actions in real time, grounded in the proven resolution patterns established in earlier phases.

When only a short issue description is available, SYNaTRA searches Jira and historical Salesforce Cases using keyword-based queries. It ranks and summarizes likely matches and proposes next steps, even before a specific customer is identified.

CS Ops teams use SYNaTRA’s structured outputs—customer overview, case list, Jira issue list, recommended resolution paths, and feedback signals—to design standard playbooks and improve coaching around recurring issue patterns.
