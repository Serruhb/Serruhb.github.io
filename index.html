INTRO

How I think about systems, automation, and impact.

I’m a Senior AI and Automation Engineer who designs systems that turn scattered data into clear, reliable decisions. My work lives at the intersection of enterprise platforms, automation, and human workflows, where small errors can quietly compound and where good systems make teams faster, calmer, and more effective.

My background in Mathematics and Chemistry shaped how I approach engineering: I look for structure, invariants, and failure modes before I reach for tools. Programming became the natural extension of that mindset—an expressive way to model complex systems and make them observable, testable, and safe to operate at scale.

Before working in enterprise automation, I built and ran a small dog training business and worked as a tutor, using early automation to track outcomes, manage operations, and reduce manual effort. Those experiences grounded my engineering instincts in real-world constraints: unclear ownership, noisy data, and systems that have to work for humans—not just on paper.

Today, I build AI-assisted and rule-driven systems that emphasize guardrails, auditability, and human-in-the-loop control—especially in high-stakes areas like revenue, customer experience, and operational decision-making. I believe great automation doesn’t replace people; it gives them better context, earlier signals, and the confidence to act.
